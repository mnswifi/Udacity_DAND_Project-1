Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row. ScheduledDay tells us on what day the patient set up their appointment. Neighborhood indicates the location of the hospital. Scholarship indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Famlia.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

Question 1: What's the distribution of Age and gender for the keeping up to scheduled appointment.

Question 2: Which of the the neighbourhoud has the highest keeping to appointment and the neighbourhood with highest missed appointment.

Question 3: Which sickness are predominant in people missing or keeping up with appointment.

Question 4: What factors are important for us to know in order to predict if a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment (x = scholarship, sms. y = No-show-appointment?

def neigh_plot(y):
    # plotting for appointments with respect to Neighourhood
    df_app[y].neighbourhood.value_counts().head(10).plot(kind="bar",figsize=(10,5)); # plotting for the first 10 topmost neighbourhood
    if (y == df_showed).any():
        plt.title("Neighbourhood with most patients keeping to appointment\n",fontsize=22);
    else:
        plt.title("Neighbourhood with most patients missing appointment\n",fontsize=22);
    plt.ylabel("Patients",fontsize=18);
    plt.xlabel("\nNeighbourhood",fontsize=18);

Conclusions¶

In conclusion, our studies shows there are more patients keeping up to appointments of which there are more females in the age group of 3-39. Bar graphs was used to ascertain this visulaization. JARDIM CAMBURI is the neighbourhood with the highest missed and patients keeping to appointments making 7% of the populations of our investigative study. Hypertension appears to be dorminant amongst patients using measure of central tendency (mean) to obtain the frequency,receiving sms and or scholarhip doesn't have significant effect in classifying turnout of patients based on the dataset of our study.
